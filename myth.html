🕵‍♂️ Debunking Civic Myths

Fact: Citizens can bring change through voting, public participation, activism, and legal actions like PILs.

Fact: Any citizen who meets the eligibility criteria can contest. Wealth is not a requirement.

Fact: Rights are subject to reasonable restrictions for public order, morality, and national security.

Fact: Many elections have been decided by narrow margins. Collective voting drives real impact.

Fact: Anyone — including students and youth — can report civic issues, write to representatives, and participate in public campaigns. Age doesn't restrict your voice.

Fact: Even if detained, you retain rights like legal representation, protection from torture, and the presumption of innocence until proven guilty.

Fact: India’s Constitution safeguards the rights of all individuals, including minorities, marginalized groups, and vulnerable communities.

Fact: Rights can evolve through amendments, judicial interpretations, and progressive policies — reflecting societal changes and values.
