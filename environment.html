🌿 Environmental Rights & Civic Duties

Protecting the environment is not just a responsibilityâ€”it's a fundamental right and duty of every citizen.

🌍 1. Right to a Healthy Environment

Article 21 of the Indian Constitution guarantees the Right to Life, which includes the right to a clean and healthy environment.

🏞️ 2. Protection of Forests and Wildlife

Article 48A urges the State to protect and improve the environment and safeguard wildlife and forests across the country.

👥 3. Duty of Citizens to Protect Nature

Article 51A(g) makes it every citizen's duty to protect and enhance the natural environment including rivers, lakes, forests, and wildlife.

🚫 4. Laws Against Pollution

The Air and Water Acts empower regulators to control pollution levels and penalize harmful activities that degrade nature.

♻ 5. Role of the Public

Individuals can reduce plastic use, join clean-up drives, report illegal dumping, and spread awareness of climate responsibilities.

📢 6. Legal Action & PILs

Citizens may file Public Interest Litigations to demand environmental protections or action against those violating green laws.
